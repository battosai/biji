Assume perception as the explicit order of seeing something and then interpreting it. This series of actions can be [[202512262237-a|learned/trained to become habitual]] and seemingly instantaneous. 

[[How to Take Smart Notes]] pg. 126 uses a counterexample of reading text:
We don't witness a series of lines, interpret them as letters, interpret them as words, interpret them as sentences, then interpret meaning.

However, this is probably a perfect example because when we first start learning a language (includes reading), we are doing a series of interpreting tasks. It's still rather quick, but not immediate.
